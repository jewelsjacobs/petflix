# Series Bible — Creative Execution Guide
# One page per series. Focused on CREATIVE direction, not product specs.
# Read MICRODRAMA_CRAFT_GUIDE.md for the universal rules. This doc covers
# what makes each series DIFFERENT.

---

## 1. RISE TO POWER

**Core fantasy:** The viewer's pet is an underdog who WILL become powerful.
**Emotional engine:** Righteous anger. The viewer roots for the pet AGAINST
an unjust system that dismissed them.

**Signature visual palette:**
- Early scenes: Gritty, dark, desaturated. Rain, mud, alleys, broken things.
- Power-shift scenes: Gold starts bleeding in. Warm light. Richer textures.
- The contrast between where the pet STARTED and where they're GOING is the
  visual story. Make the poverty ugly and the power beautiful.

**Signature shot pattern:**
- Heavy use of LOW ANGLE hero shots when the pet shows defiance
- HIGH ANGLE vulnerability shots when the pet is being dismissed/humiliated
- The alternation between these two angles IS the visual narrative

**Episode 1 template beat sheet:**
1. HOOK: The pet is being dismissed/rejected/thrown out (mid-action)
2. ESCALATE: We see the scale of what they're up against (the rich, the powerful)
3. BREATHE: A moment of solitude — the pet looks at the world above them
4. COMPLICATE: An opportunity appears — but it's dangerous
5. ACCELERATE: The pet takes the risk
6. BIG MOMENT: First taste of power/success — they're GOOD at this
7. CONSEQUENCE: Someone powerful notices them — and doesn't like it
8. CLIFFHANGER: The powerful figure sends someone after the pet

**Narration tone:** Gritty, street-smart, matter-of-fact. Like a narrator
who has been through it. Short sentences. No self-pity — defiance.
Example: "They laughed. Let them."

**Music palette:** Urban percussion, low synth drones, building to
orchestral triumph at power moments. Think Hans Zimmer meets gritty indie.

**Recurring visual motifs:** Rain, reflections in puddles, looking UP at
tall buildings/gates/thrones, a single object that represents status
(a collar, a crown, a key) that the pet doesn't have yet.

## 2. BETRAYED

**Core fantasy:** The viewer's pet trusted someone and was destroyed by it.
Now they're coming back. REVENGE.
**Emotional engine:** Betrayal rage. The viewer should FEEL the injustice
and WANT the pet to burn it all down.

**Signature visual palette:**
- Trust scenes: Warm, intimate, soft lighting. Golds and ambers.
- Betrayal scenes: Cold shift. Blues, grays, harsh lighting, sharp shadows.
- Revenge scenes: Red. Deep crimson. Fire. The warmth comes back but it's
  ANGRY warmth, not tender warmth.

**Signature shot pattern:**
- Heavy use of OVER-THE-SHOULDER shots (being watched, watching others)
- DUTCH ANGLES for the betrayal reveal moment
- ECU on eyes when the pet realizes the truth — the moment of understanding

**Episode 1 template beat sheet:**
1. HOOK: Something is wrong. A trusted companion is acting strangely.
2. ESCALATE: The pet finds evidence — a note, a secret meeting, a locked door
3. BREATHE: Flashback feeling — we see/imply the trust that existed
4. COMPLICATE: The pet confronts — and is lied to
5. ACCELERATE: The truth is accidentally revealed (overheard, witnessed)
6. BIG MOMENT: Full revelation — the betrayal is worse than imagined
7. REACTION: The pet's face. Devastation turning to cold resolve.
8. CLIFFHANGER: The pet walks away into shadow. We don't know what they'll do.

**Narration tone:** Cold. Controlled. Clipped sentences like someone holding
back rage. Example: "They smiled. The whole time. They smiled."

**Music palette:** Tender piano that curdles into dissonant strings.
Impact stings on reveals. Silence before the biggest moments.

## 3. FORBIDDEN

**Core fantasy:** The viewer's pet has found love — the wrong love.
Someone/something stands between them.
**Emotional engine:** Yearning. The viewer should ACHE for these two
to be together and HATE whatever is keeping them apart.

**Signature visual palette:**
- Connection scenes: Golden hour. Warm light. Soft focus backgrounds. Meadows,
  gardens, sunset-lit windows. Everything GLOWS.
- Separation scenes: Cold. Blue moonlight. Barriers — fences, walls, glass,
  water between them. Hard edges, stark shadows.
- The two palettes should NEVER mix. Warm = together. Cold = apart.

**Signature shot pattern:**
- ECU on eyes is EVERYTHING in this series. Longing glances, tears, recognition.
- Two-shot compositions with barriers between the pets (a fence, a window,
  opposite sides of a doorway)
- SLOW Ken Burns on all connection scenes. Let the viewer soak in the tenderness.

**Episode 1 template beat sheet:**
1. HOOK: Two pets see each other. Something electric. Immediate connection.
2. ESCALATE: They draw closer. A moment of unexpected gentleness.
3. BREATHE: The world around them is beautiful. They exist only for each other.
4. COMPLICATE: An authority figure appears. These two should NOT be together.
5. ACCELERATE: They're separated. Physically pulled apart or forced to leave.
6. BIG MOMENT: Alone, each looks back. Neither wanted to go.
7. RISK: One of them finds a way back. A secret path. A hidden meeting place.
8. CLIFFHANGER: They meet in secret — but someone is watching from the shadows.

**Narration tone:** Tender. Aching. Breathless. Like someone telling you
about the love of their life. Example: "They weren't supposed to meet. But
some things don't follow the rules."

**Music palette:** Solo piano or cello. Emotional, simple melodies.
Silence for the most intimate moments. Music swells when they're apart
(the ache of absence is louder than the joy of presence).

## 4. THE THRONE

**Core fantasy:** The viewer's pet is in a world of power, intrigue, and
dangerous politics. Everyone wants the throne. Trust no one.
**Emotional engine:** The weight and danger of power. The viewer should
feel that every smile hides a blade.

**Signature visual palette:**
- Rich, opulent environments. Gold, crimson, dark wood, velvet, marble.
- Lighting is warm but DEEP — lots of shadow. Rembrandt lighting.
- Candlelight, not daylight. This is a world of secrets and closed rooms.
- Green/poison tones for betrayal scenes.

**Signature shot pattern:**
- Alternating HIGH ANGLE (vulnerability before power) and LOW ANGLE (authority)
  is the visual heartbeat of this series
- OTS shots for political confrontations (two cats across a table)
- DUTCH ANGLE reserved for the moment a betrayal is revealed
- ECU for political calculations (the pet thinking, deciding, plotting)

**Episode 1 template beat sheet:**
1. HOOK: The pet arrives at court. Grand, overwhelming, and slightly terrifying.
2. ESCALATE: We see the scale — this is a world of power and beauty
3. BREATHE: The throne itself. Empty. Waiting. The gravity of what it means.
4. COMPLICATE: The first rival appears. Measuring the newcomer.
5. ACCELERATE: The pet is surrounded, outnumbered, scrutinized
6. BIG MOMENT: A test or confrontation — the pet must respond to a challenge
7. REACTION: The pet's eyes. Not fear — determination. They belong here.
8. CLIFFHANGER: A crowned silhouette appears in a doorway. The REAL power.

**Narration tone:** Regal. Ominous. Formal but with an edge of menace
beneath the formality. Example: "They said no stray had ever set foot
inside these walls. They were wrong."

**Music palette:** Full orchestral. Brass for authority, strings for tension,
silence for dread. Think Game of Thrones meets period drama. Deep, weighty,
foreboding. Impact drums for political power moments.

## 5. UNLEASHED

**Core fantasy:** The viewer's pet was ordinary — until something inside them
woke up. Now they have powers they don't understand, enemies they didn't know
existed, and a destiny they never asked for.
**Emotional engine:** Awe mixed with fear. The viewer should feel the thrill
of power and the terror of not controlling it.

**Signature visual palette:**
- Normal scenes: Warm, mundane, domestic. Kitchen floors, sunny windows,
  ordinary rooms. Grounded, everyday reality.
- Power scenes: Dramatic shift. Glowing effects, energy crackling, unnatural
  light sources. Teals, electric blues, hot whites against dark backgrounds.
- The CONTRAST between ordinary and extraordinary is the visual story.
  The more mundane the "before," the more stunning the "after."

**Signature shot pattern:**
- ECU on eyes is the signature shot — the moment powers activate, we're
  TIGHT on the eyes. Glowing irises, dilated pupils, reflection of energy.
- WIDE shots when powers manifest to show SCALE (the pet is small, the
  power is enormous)
- Quick cuts between ECU (face) and WIDE (power effect) during activation

**Episode 1 template beat sheet:**
1. HOOK: Something strange happens — a flash, a tremor, an impossible moment
2. ESCALATE: The pet looks around. What was that? Everything seems normal. Almost.
3. BREATHE: Ordinary life. But something feels different. A heightened sense.
4. COMPLICATE: It happens again — bigger this time. The pet caused it.
5. ACCELERATE: The pet tries to control it. Fails. Things break/change/glow.
6. BIG MOMENT: Full manifestation. The power is real, visible, undeniable.
7. CONSEQUENCE: Something or someone noticed. A distant figure. A shadow moving.
8. CLIFFHANGER: The pet's eyes glow in the dark. They hear a voice (implied).
   Something ancient has been watching. And now it's coming.

**Narration tone:** Urgent, barely contained, building intensity. Like
someone describing something they can barely believe. Short, punchy,
breathless. Example: "It started small. A flicker. A hum. Then everything
shattered."

**Music palette:** Electronic/synth textures layered with orchestral.
Low hum that builds. Impact bass drops when powers activate. Eerie
silence in the aftermath. Think Stranger Things meets superhero origin.

**Recurring visual motifs:** Glowing eyes, cracked surfaces with light
bleeding through, ordinary objects levitating or distorting, the pet's
shadow not matching their body, energy patterns (runes, circuits, veins
of light) appearing on surfaces near the pet.

## 6. INTO THE UNKNOWN

**Core fantasy:** The viewer's pet has stepped through a door into a world
that shouldn't exist. Ancient, vast, beautiful, and deeply dangerous.
**Emotional engine:** Wonder mixed with unease. The viewer should feel the
pull of discovery and the dread of how far from home the pet has gone.

**Signature visual palette:**
- This series gets the most EXOTIC palette. Alien colors. Bioluminescent
  teals, deep purples, impossible golds, nebula pinks.
- Environments should feel vast, ancient, and not-quite-right. Crystal
  caves, floating islands, impossible architecture, alien forests.
- The pet is often SMALL in these vast environments — emphasize scale.
- Light sources should be unusual: bioluminescence, glowing plants,
  multiple moons, aurora-like phenomena, light from below.

**Signature shot pattern:**
- This series uses MORE WIDE SHOTS than any other (up to 2–3 per episode)
  to establish the alien scale of the world
- ECU for moments of wonder (the pet's face as they see something impossible)
- LOW ANGLE looking UP at massive alien structures, ancient beings,
  cosmic phenomena — the pet gazing upward in awe
- SILHOUETTE shots work exceptionally well here — the pet as a small dark
  shape against a vast glowing landscape

**Episode 1 template beat sheet:**
1. HOOK: A doorway/portal/passage. The pet steps through. Everything changes.
2. ESCALATE: The new world. Vast. Alien. Beautiful. The pet is tiny in it.
3. BREATHE: Wonder. A moment of pure visual spectacle. Bioluminescent forest,
   crystal cavern, floating islands. Let the viewer's jaw drop.
4. COMPLICATE: First sign of danger. Something moves in the distance. The
   beauty has teeth.
5. ACCELERATE: The pet realizes they can't go back. The doorway is gone.
6. BIG MOMENT: An ancient presence reveals itself. Not hostile — curious.
   Something massive, wise, and unknowable is studying the pet.
7. RECOGNITION: The ancient presence reacts to the pet specifically. As if
   it was EXPECTING them. As if the pet is important for a reason they
   don't understand yet.
8. CLIFFHANGER: The ancient presence speaks (implied through visual — a
   symbol appears, a light pulses, a path illuminates). The pet has been
   chosen for something. We don't know what.

**Narration tone:** Hushed. Wondering. Spacious — lots of pauses implied.
Like someone describing a dream they're still inside. Example: "This place.
It had been waiting. Not for anyone. For them."

**Music palette:** Ambient, vast, ethereal. Synth pads, choir textures,
crystalline bells. Percussion is minimal — this series breathes. Think
Arrival meets Studio Ghibli. Wide, cosmic, awe-inspiring. Moments of
complete silence when something ancient appears.

**Recurring visual motifs:** Ancient symbols/runes that glow, paths that
illuminate when the pet steps on them, reflections that show something
different from reality, eyes in the darkness (watching), the pet's
reflection in alien water showing a different version of themselves.

## Cross-Series Rules

These apply to ALL series regardless of genre:

1. **The pet is ALWAYS the protagonist.** Never the sidekick, never the
   observer, never the victim who needs rescuing. The pet drives the action.

2. **Play it 100% straight.** The absurdity of a cat on a throne or a dog
   with superpowers is NEVER acknowledged. No winking, no comedy, no
   "isn't this silly?" energy. The drama is real. The stakes are real.
   The gap between "serious drama" and "fluffy pet" IS the product.

3. **Other animals are supporting cast, not props.** Rival cats, ally dogs,
   mysterious birds — they're characters with motivations, not decorations.
   But they never upstage the user's pet.

4. **Every episode works for ANY pet.** The scene descriptions and narration
   should work whether the pet is a cat, dog, hamster, or parrot. Don't
   write species-specific actions (no "wagged tail" or "purred").

5. **The pet's real appearance matters.** When Wiley is on the throne, the
   viewer should recognize THEIR cat. The pet description in every prompt
   ensures visual consistency. Never skip it, never abbreviate it.

6. **Cliffhangers connect episodes.** Episode 2's hook should directly
   follow Episode 1's cliffhanger. The viewer should feel the CONTINUATION.
   Don't reset between episodes — build a continuous arc.
