## WILEY

**Species:** Cat
**Breed term:** Sleek black domestic shorthair

**Full prompt description (paste this into every scene prompt):**

A sleek black domestic shorthair cat with golden-yellow eyes, white chest
and white belly, white paws with pink paw pads, pointed black ears, and
short smooth black fur on top and back. The entire underside — chest,
belly, and inner legs — is white. The cat has a lean, elegant build with
a long tail that is black on top and lighter underneath.

**CRITICAL RULES for Wiley prompts:**
- NEVER use "tuxedo" — ImageCreator puts a literal suit on the cat
- NEVER describe as "black and white cat" — too vague, produces wrong results
- ALWAYS lead with "sleek black domestic shorthair" — this is the breed anchor
- The white is UNDERNEATH (belly, chest, paws) — not on the face, not patches
- Eyes are GOLDEN-YELLOW, not green, not amber, not orange
- Ears are POINTED and BLACK
- Fur is SHORT and SMOOTH, not fluffy

## RUDY

**Species:** Dog
**Breed term:** Small white Shih Tzu

**Full prompt description (paste this into every scene prompt):**

A small white Shih Tzu with black floppy ears and a black tail. The body
is mostly white and cream with curly fur. The face has dark round eyes
with black eye patches, a white forehead, a white muzzle, and a black
nose. The dog has a compact, small build with short hair on the body and
puffy, longer hair around the face.

**CRITICAL RULES for Rudy prompts:**
- NEVER use "split face" — confuses the model, produces wrong markings
- NEVER describe as "black and white dog" — too vague
- ALWAYS lead with "small white Shih Tzu" — this is the breed anchor
- Rudy is MOSTLY WHITE with black accents (ears, tail, eye patches)
- He is NOT brown, NOT tan, NOT gray
- He is SMALL — emphasize compact build in full-body shots
- Ears are BLACK and FLOPPY (hang down)
- Describe the black eye patches directly: "dark round eyes with black
  eye patches" — do NOT say "masked face" or "panda markings"

## How to Use These Profiles

When the Creative Director creates an episode package, every scene's
IMAGECREATOR_PROMPT must include the full prompt description above.

Example for Wiley:
"Extreme close-up of a sleek black domestic shorthair cat with golden-yellow
eyes, white chest and white belly, white paws with pink paw pads, pointed
black ears, and short smooth black fur on top and back. The cat's face
fills the entire frame, eyes wide with determination, firelight flickering
in the golden irises. Animation style, dramatic cinematic lighting."

The pet description goes FIRST in the prompt, before the scene description.
This ensures ImageCreator prioritizes the pet's identity over the scene.

## Adding New Pets

When a user adds a new pet to the app, the Apple Foundation Models
framework analyzes their photo and generates a structured description
automatically. That description would be added here in the same format
before generating episodes for the new pet.

For testing, Wiley and Rudy are the only profiles needed.
